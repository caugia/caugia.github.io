1. Before You Begin

How long does the assessment take?
Most people complete the assessment in approximately 35 minutes. If you already know your core metrics (ARR, win rate, churn, NRR, burn rate), it may go faster. You can pause and resume at any time.
Can I complete it in multiple sessions?
Yes. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser as you answer questions. You can close the page and return at any time. Use your access code to get back to the assessment.
Do I need access to CRM or analytics tools?
Not necessarily, but it helps. Some questions ask for specific metrics, and having your CRM or analytics dashboard available will make the process faster and more accurate.
What information should I have ready?
Having a few core metrics available makes the experience smoother:
  • ARR, ACV, and revenue targets
  • CAC, LTV, NRR, churn rate
  • Win rate, sales cycle length, pipeline coverage
  • Team sizes across marketing, sales, CS, and RevOps
  • Monthly burn rate and gross margin
Directional numbers are fine. Perfect precision is not required.
Who should complete the assessment?
Ideally a revenue leader with visibility into your Go-to-Market system and performance metrics. Many companies involve their CEO or Finance for specific inputs such as monthly burn, active customers, or gross margin. The assessment should be completed by one person to ensure consistency. You can share the final report with your team.

2. Metric Definitions

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
Total annualized value of all active recurring subscriptions. Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) multiplied by 12. Exclude one-time fees, professional services, and non-recurring revenue.
YoY Growth Rate
Year-over-year revenue growth expressed as a percentage. Formula: ((Current ARR - ARR 12 months ago) / ARR 12 months ago) x 100.
NRR (Net Revenue Retention)
Revenue retained from existing customers after accounting for expansion, contraction, and churn. Formula: ((Starting ARR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting ARR) x 100. Target: 110%+ for healthy SaaS.
Gross Margin
Revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS), expressed as a percentage of revenue. Formula: ((Revenue - COGS) / Revenue) x 100. COGS typically includes hosting, support, and customer success costs directly tied to delivery.
Monthly Net Burn
Total monthly cash outflow minus total monthly cash inflow. If you spend more than you earn, the result is negative (burn). Include all operating expenses — payroll, tools, rent, marketing spend.
Cash Runway
Number of months your company can operate at the current burn rate before running out of cash. Formula: Total cash balance / Monthly net burn.
Win Rate
Percentage of qualified opportunities that close as won. Formula: (Won deals / Total qualified opportunities) x 100. Only count opportunities that entered the pipeline as qualified — exclude disqualified or junk leads.
Sales Cycle
Average number of days from opportunity creation to closed-won. Measured across all won deals in a given period. Exclude outliers (e.g. deals that sat dormant for months).
Pipeline Coverage Ratio
Total pipeline value divided by the revenue target for the period. Formula: Open pipeline / Revenue target. A 3x ratio means you have three times more pipeline than your target. Typical healthy range: 3x to 4x.
Churn Rate (Annual)
Percentage of ARR lost from customers who cancelled or did not renew in a 12-month period. Formula: (Churned ARR / Starting ARR) x 100. This is gross churn — it does not account for expansion revenue.
CAC Payback Period
Number of months to recover the cost of acquiring a customer. Formula: CAC / (Monthly revenue per customer x Gross margin %). If your CAC is $30,000 and each customer generates $2,500/month at 75% margin, payback is 16 months.
LTV:CAC Ratio
Customer lifetime value divided by customer acquisition cost. Formula: LTV / CAC. LTV = Average revenue per customer x Gross margin x Average customer lifetime. Healthy target: 3x or higher.
Quota Attainment
Percentage of sales reps who hit or exceeded their quota in the last completed quarter. Formula: (Number of reps at or above quota / Total quota-carrying reps) x 100.
Revenue Concentration
Percentage of total revenue coming from your top 10 customers. High concentration (above 30-40%) signals customer dependency risk.
ACV (Average Contract Value)
Average annualized value of a new customer contract. Formula: Total new business ARR / Number of new customers in the period.
Sales and Marketing spend as % of revenue
Total sales and marketing costs (people, tools, programs, ads) divided by total revenue, expressed as a percentage. Includes headcount costs for all GTM roles.

3. Completing the Assessment

How do I save my progress?
Your progress is saved automatically every few seconds using your browser's local storage. You do not need to press a save button. There is also a manual "Save Progress" button at the top of the assessment if you want to be sure.
Can I go back and change answers?
Yes. Use the "Previous" button to navigate to earlier questions and update any answer before submitting. You can also use the pillar navigation on the right to jump to any section.
What if I do not know an answer?
Choose the option that best approximates your current situation. The first option in each question typically represents the lowest maturity level (e.g. "No strategy", "No insight", "No tracking"). Selecting these is diagnostically meaningful — it tells the engine where visibility or capability gaps exist. Perfect precision is not required.
How precise do my answers need to be?
They do not need to be perfect. Directional accuracy is enough (e.g. "around 5% churn" rather than "4.8%"). If you cannot estimate at all, choose the fallback option.
Why can I not leave questions blank?
Blank answers provide no diagnostic value. The engine needs to know why data is missing (e.g. not tracked vs not applicable) to build your maturity profile correctly. Every option, including "I don't know," carries diagnostic weight.
What do the "First Unanswered" and "Last Answered" buttons do?
"First Unanswered" jumps to the first question you have not answered yet. "Last Answered" jumps to the most recent question you completed. Both are useful when resuming after a break.

4. Understanding the Questions

What does a maturity level represent?
Maturity levels describe how structured, reliable, and consistent a capability is. They range from reactive (dependent on individual effort) to predictable (system-driven and repeatable).
Are there right or wrong answers?
No. There is only an accurate reflection of your current state. Being honest about gaps is the only way to get a diagnosis that actually identifies your real constraints.
What if none of the options describe our situation perfectly?
Choose the option that best approximates your reality. GTM environments are complex. Pick the answer that is most true for your primary revenue motion.
What are the 12 pillars?
The assessment evaluates your GTM system across 12 pillars organized in the GRIP Framework:
  • Guidance: GTM Strategy and Leadership, Market Intelligence, Product Marketing
  • Resources: Pricing and Packaging, Product Readiness, Enablement
  • Implementation: Demand Generation, Sales Execution, Revenue Operations
  • Performance: Customer Success and Expansion, Data and Insights, Alignment and Governance

5. Technical Issues

My answers are not saving.
Check that you are not in Private or Incognito mode, as this blocks local storage. Switch to a regular browser window to ensure progress is saved. Also make sure you are using a modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge).
The page froze. Will I lose my progress?
Unlikely. Because progress saves continuously, you can refresh the page or close and reopen the tab without losing your work.
Does this work on mobile?
Yes, but for the best experience we recommend completing the assessment on a desktop or tablet. You will need to reference metrics and data, which is easier on a larger screen.
I cleared my browser data. Is my progress lost?
Yes. Clearing browser data (cookies and local storage) removes saved progress. If this happens, you will need to start the assessment again. We recommend not clearing browser data until after you have submitted.
The "Reset" button — what does it do?
The Reset button clears all your answers and starts the assessment from the beginning. Use it only if you intentionally want to start over. This action cannot be undone.

6. After Submission

When will I receive my report?
Your 45-page GTM Intelligence Report is generated and delivered to your inbox within one hour after submission. No scheduling, no consultant readout, no follow-up calls.
What format is the report?
The report is delivered as a PDF document by email. It contains a 45-page board-level analysis including GRIP scores, constraint analysis, revenue leakage modelling, a System Failure Map, and a 90-day execution sequence.
Can I share the report with my team?
Yes. The report is designed to be shared with your leadership team and used as a foundation for strategic decisions. It is a PDF that you can forward or present.
Can I review my answers after submission?
No. After submission, the assessment is closed and inputs are locked to preserve diagnostic integrity. Your answers are processed by the Constraint Engine and reflected in the final report. If you want to revisit your inputs, we recommend taking notes as you go.
Can I retake the assessment later?
Each access code is valid for one assessment submission. If you want to run the diagnostic again (e.g. after 6 months), you would purchase a new Intelligence Report.
