1. Before You Begin

How long does the assessment take to complete? ▼
Most people complete the assessment in 45–60 minutes. If you already know your core metrics (CAC, NRR, win rates, pipeline coverage), it may go faster.
Can I complete it in multiple sessions? ▼
Yes. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser as you answer questions. You can close the page and return at any time.
Do I need access to CRM or analytics tools while answering? ▼
Not necessarily, but it helps. Some questions ask for specific metrics, and having your CRM or analytics dashboard available will make the process faster and more accurate.
What information should I have ready before starting? ▼
A simple cheat sheet makes the experience smoother.

Recommended:
  • ARR, ACV and revenue targets
  • CAC, LTV, NRR, churn rate
  • Win rate, sales cycle length, pipeline coverage
  • Team sizes across marketing, sales, CS and RevOps
  • Conversion rates (MQL→SQL, SQL→Opportunity, Opportunity→Won)
  • YoY growth, expansion rate
  • Any known GTM bottlenecks
You do not need perfect precision. Directional numbers are fine.
Should multiple people fill this in together or individually? ▼
Either works. Some leaders fill it in solo with data at hand. Others complete it with RevOps or the broader GTM leadership team to ensure full alignment.
Can I delegate parts of the assessment to teammates? ▼
This is possible but not recommended. The assessment is designed to be completed by one person with broad GTM visibility. Splitting ownership across individuals can lead to inconsistent responses.

2. Completing the Assessment

How do I save my progress? ▼
Your progress is saved automatically every few seconds. You can also click "Save" if you want to ensure your latest changes are stored before closing the page.
Can I pause and resume later? ▼
Yes. Your progress is stored locally in your browser. When you return, you will continue exactly where you left off.
Can I go back and change answers? ▼
Yes. You can navigate freely between completed pillars and update any answer before submitting the assessment.
Can I skip questions and come back later? ▼
You can move forward without answering, but you cannot submit the assessment with unanswered questions. When you return to a question, you will always have structured fallback options available.
What happens if I close the page? ▼
Nothing is lost. Your progress is saved locally and will reload when you return.
What if I don't know an answer? ▼
You will never be forced to guess. Every question includes fallback options that allow you to continue without leaving anything blank. These include:
  • We do not track this yet
  • We do this inconsistently
  • Limited visibility / unsure
  • Handled manually, not systematized
  • No defined process
  • Not applicable to our GTM motion
Choose the option that best matches your situation. These answers are diagnostically meaningful and help us understand your maturity level accurately.
How precise do my answers need to be? ▼
They do not need to be perfect. Directional accuracy is enough — for example, providing 5% churn when the real number is between 4% and 6% is completely fine. If you cannot estimate with confidence, choose the appropriate fallback option.
What if we don't track this metric yet? ▼
Select "We do not track this yet." This is a valid and important signal — it tells us something meaningful about data maturity, not performance.
Should I estimate or choose a fallback option? ▼
If you can estimate reasonably, estimate. If you cannot, choose the fallback option. Both approaches are perfectly acceptable.
Why can't I leave questions blank? ▼
Blank answers provide no diagnostic value. Fallback options allow us to understand why the information is missing, which is essential for an accurate GTM maturity profile.
Will my answers be visible to my team or colleagues? ▼
No. Your responses are private and visible only to you and Caugia Consulting, unless you choose to share the final report yourself.

3. Understanding the Questions

What does a "maturity level" represent? ▼
Maturity levels describe how structured, reliable and consistent a capability is within your GTM system. They range from reactive and inconsistent to predictable and fully architected.
Are there right or wrong answers? ▼
No. There is no "correct" answer — only an accurate reflection of your current state. Choosing fallback options (e.g., "We do not track this yet") is just as valid as entering exact metrics.
Why do some questions feel similar? ▼
GTM systems overlap. For example, forecasting touches both Sales Execution and Revenue Operations. Similar-looking questions help triangulate a reliable diagnostic signal across multiple pillars.
What if none of the provided options describe our situation perfectly? ▼
Choose the option that best approximates reality. GTM environments are rarely binary or perfect matches.
What if different teams work differently across the organization? ▼
Answer based on what is most common or representative. If operations differ significantly by region, choose the state that reflects your primary or most impactful segment.

4. Technical Issues

My answers aren't saving — what should I do? ▼
Check that your browser allows local storage. Private/incognito mode may prevent saving. Use a regular window or switch to another supported browser.
The page froze or crashed — will I lose progress? ▼
Unlikely. Because progress is saved continuously, you can refresh or reopen the page without losing your work.
I clicked Reset by accident — can this be undone? ▼
No. Reset permanently clears all saved progress. If this happens, you will need to restart the assessment.
The Next or Previous buttons aren't working. ▼
This may indicate a required question was left unanswered. Fill in the missing field or choose a fallback option.
Dark mode isn't working. ▼
This may be due to cache or an unsupported browser extension. Try refreshing the page or switching to a supported browser.
Does this work on mobile? ▼
Yes. However, for the best experience — especially when referencing metrics — we recommend completing the assessment on a desktop or tablet.
Which browsers are supported? ▼
Chrome, Safari, Firefox and Edge (current versions). Internet Explorer is not supported.

5. After Submission

When will I receive my GTM Intelligence Report? ▼
Within 5–7 business days. The report is built manually using your responses and our GTM Framework diagnostic engine.
What happens immediately after I submit? ▼
You receive a confirmation email. Our team begins analyzing your responses and mapping them to the 12 GTM pillars.
Can I download or revisit my answers later? ▼
After submission, you cannot edit your responses. If needed, you may request a copy of your raw data from our support team.
Will I see my GTM Score right away? ▼
No. Your score is calculated as part of the full analysis and included in the final report.
Can I share my report with my team? ▼
Yes. The report is yours to distribute to your leadership team, board, advisors or investors.
What format will the report be in? ▼
You receive a structured PDF with:
  • your GTM Score
  • pillar-by-pillar maturity breakdown
  • archetype classification
  • insights, risks, bottlenecks
  • recommendations and GTM priorities
How are my answers analyzed? ▼
Your responses are evaluated against our GTM Framework maturity model, which analyzes:
  • structure
  • consistency
  • predictability
  • alignment
  • data foundations
Each pillar receives a maturity score and contributes to your overall GTM Score.
Is my data stored securely? ▼
Yes. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest and never shared with third parties. Aggregated, anonymized insights may be used to improve the diagnostic model.
Can I redo the assessment in the future? ▼
Yes. Many clients retake the assessment after 6–12 months to measure progress and maturity improvements.
