Product Readiness — FAQ

We keep losing deals to “product gaps.” Will this tell us if it is really the product or a positioning problem?

Yes. The Product-Market Fit engine measures whether your product actually solves validated buyer problems, while the UX & Adoption engine checks if the product delivers value users can access. The diagnostic cross-references your technical validation loss rate against product readiness scores. If your readiness score is above 55 but you are losing on “product gaps,” the problem is likely positioning, not product.

What benchmarks does this module compare us against?

Product readiness score target of 55 or above, technical validation loss rate, product-to-GTM headcount ratio of at least 0.15, platform reliability metrics, and integration ecosystem completeness. A product:GTM ratio below 0.1 flags that engineering is stretched too thin to support the GTM motion. The diagnostic also measures development velocity, UX adoption depth, and product analytics maturity.

What specific insights will the diagnostic reveal?

Six engines evaluate product from a GTM perspective: Product-Market Fit measures whether PMF is validated or assumed. UX & Adoption evaluates whether users can actually access the product’s value. Development Velocity assesses whether engineering delivers at the cadence GTM needs. Platform Reliability measures uptime, performance, and technical debt impact. Integration evaluates ecosystem completeness and API maturity. Product Analytics scores whether product data informs GTM decisions or sits unused.

How is this different from a product roadmap review or technical assessment?

A roadmap review looks forward at what you plan to build. A technical assessment evaluates code quality. This diagnostic measures whether your current product supports the GTM motion you are running today — whether deals are lost to real product gaps or perception gaps, whether engineering velocity matches market demands, and whether your integration ecosystem is a competitive advantage or a liability. It is a GTM-focused product evaluation, not a technical audit.

Can 60 questions really assess product readiness for GTM?

The 6-engine architecture evaluates product from every GTM-relevant angle: fit, usability, velocity, reliability, integration, and analytics. Each engine has 10 questions calibrated against B2B SaaS benchmarks. The per-engine breakdown reveals whether your product constraint is PMF validation, UX friction, engineering bandwidth, platform stability, integration gaps, or missing product analytics — each requiring very different investments.
