Revenue Operations. FAQ

Our forecast misses every quarter. Will this tell us if RevOps is the bottleneck?

Yes. The Forecasting engine evaluates methodology rigor, variance tracking, and whether your forecasting process is a structured discipline or a late-week guessing exercise. The CRM & Data engine then checks if the underlying data is even trustworthy. forecast accuracy depends on CRM hygiene. When forecast accuracy falls below 70%, the diagnostic flags it as below effectiveness threshold and traces the root cause across both engines.

What benchmarks does this module compare us against?

CRM data quality target above 80%, forecast accuracy within 15% variance, pipeline stage definitions with verifiable exit criteria, and process automation coverage. Forecast accuracy below 70% is flagged as below effectiveness. CRM hygiene below 80% means your data is unreliable for any downstream analysis. The diagnostic also measures tech stack integration depth and governance maturity.

What specific insights will the diagnostic reveal?

Six engines cover the RevOps foundation: CRM & Data measures data quality, architecture, and hygiene discipline. Tech Stack evaluates integration completeness and tool rationalization. Process Automation scores operational efficiency and manual bottleneck identification. Reporting assesses whether leadership has reliable, timely dashboards. Forecasting measures methodology rigor and variance tracking. Governance evaluates change management, documentation, and operational standards.

How is this different from a Salesforce admin audit or tech stack review?

An admin audit checks configuration. A tech stack review lists your tools. This diagnostic measures whether your RevOps infrastructure actually enables the GTM motion. whether data quality supports forecasting, whether processes are automated or manual, whether reporting gives leadership actionable answers, and whether governance prevents drift. It diagnoses operational effectiveness, not just technical correctness.

Can 60 questions really assess our RevOps maturity?

The 6-engine architecture covers data through process through governance. Each engine has 10 questions calibrated against RevOps maturity benchmarks, from basic (spreadsheet-driven, reactive) to advanced (automated, governed, predictive). The per-engine breakdown means you see exactly which operational layer is the constraint. bad data, fragmented tech, manual processes, or weak governance. rather than getting a vague maturity score.
