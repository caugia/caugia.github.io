Market Intelligence. FAQ

We think we know our ICP, but win rates keep declining. Can this diagnose why?

Exactly this scenario. The ICP & Segmentation engine evaluates whether your ICP is a documented, validated definition or a tribal assumption. The Buyer Decision Mapping engine then checks if you understand how those buyers actually evaluate and purchase. When ICP clarity scores below 5 and win rate is under 20%, the diagnostic flags it as a critical gap. you are likely targeting the wrong accounts or misunderstanding how they buy.

What benchmarks does this module compare us against?

The diagnostic scores ICP clarity on a 1–10 scale, win/loss analysis maturity, competitive landscape coverage, buyer journey mapping completeness, and Voice of Customer feedback loops. The critical threshold: ICP clarity below 5 combined with win rate under 20% signals your entire GTM motion is built on unvalidated assumptions. High performers maintain documented ICPs updated quarterly with validated buyer decision criteria.

What specific insights will the diagnostic reveal?

Six engines, each targeting a distinct intelligence gap: ICP & Segmentation measures whether your targeting is validated or assumed. Buyer Decision Mapping checks if you have documented how buyers evaluate, compare, and decide. Competitive Intel assesses whether you systematically track competitor positioning and win/loss patterns. Market Trends evaluates your ability to spot category shifts early. Voice of Customer measures feedback integration from existing customers. MI Operations scores the operational rigor behind your intelligence practice.

How is this different from what our CRM or intent data tools tell us?

CRM data tells you who you are targeting. Intent tools tell you who is researching. Neither tells you whether your fundamental market assumptions are correct. This diagnostic measures structural intelligence gaps. whether your ICP definition is validated, whether your competitive intel is systematic or anecdotal, and whether buyer decision criteria actually inform your sales process. It diagnoses why your targeting fails, not just where leads come from.

Can 60 questions really assess our market intelligence maturity?

The 6-engine architecture ensures comprehensive coverage. Each engine has 10 questions calibrated against B2B SaaS benchmarks, scoring from basic (ad-hoc, tribal knowledge) to advanced (systematic, operationalized intelligence). The output is not a single score but a per-engine breakdown that shows exactly where intelligence breaks down. whether it is ICP validation, competitive tracking, or buyer journey understanding.
