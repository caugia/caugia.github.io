The GRIP Framework

Every GTM system operates across four dimensions. The Caugia Engine evaluates structural balance across all four to find where the system breaks.

G

Guidance

Is direction clear and intentional?

GTM strategy, market understanding, and product market framing.

R

Resources

Is the system properly equipped?

Pricing structure, product readiness, and enablement capacity.

I

Implementation

Is execution consistent and scalable?

Demand generation, sales execution, and revenue operations.

P

Performance

Do outcomes compound over time?

Customer expansion, data visibility, and governance alignment.

What Caugia is becoming

From diagnostics to execution governance.

Caugia began as a deterministic diagnostic engine — a way to make GTM constraints visible, measurable, and actionable without relying on consultant opinion.

It is evolving into a full GTM intelligence and execution governance system. The GTM Intelligence Report finds the constraint. GRIP OS governs what happens next — enforcing one active constraint at a time, with explicit gates, blocked actions, decision memory, and weekly executive briefings tied to financial impact.

The ambition is clear: make GTM constraints not only diagnosable, but governable. So that leadership decisions are driven by systems intelligence, not guesswork.
