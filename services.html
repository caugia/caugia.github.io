Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the GTM Score and the Intelligence Report?

The GTM Score (Free) is a quick "pulse check" to see where you stand. The Intelligence Report (€949) is a deep-dive, guided self-assessment. You answer structured strategic questions, and the system generates a board-level analysis usually only available through expensive consulting audits.

Who is this designed for?

Caugia is built for leaders who want clarity on how their revenue engine works. Typical users include CEOs, CROs, CMOs, Heads of GTM, Revenue Operations and founder-led teams. The framework adapts to different stages, from early scale to enterprise.

Do we need a certain level of GTM maturity?

No. GRIP adapts to your stage and reveals the constraints that matter now. Early stage companies use it to create structure. Larger organizations use it to align teams, strengthen operations and remove guesswork.

How long does the process take?

The free GTM Score takes a few minutes. The Intelligence Report is delivered on the same day. Modules and advisory support are added only where they create meaningful movement with minimal disruption.

Can we start with modules without doing the Score or the Report?

It is possible, but not recommended. The Score and the Intelligence Report provide the context that makes each module more precise and prevent teams from working on symptoms rather than root causes.

How does GRIP differ from other GTM frameworks?

GRIP is a structured operating system for GTM. It connects market reality, execution quality and organisational alignment in one model. Unlike channel or pipeline frameworks, GRIP evaluates the system as a whole and shows exactly where leverage sits.

What happens after we receive the results?

You receive clear recommendations, structural insights and a priority sequence. Most companies then choose targeted modules or advisory support to turn those insights into measurable improvement.

Is this suitable for companies preparing for fundraising?

Yes. GRIP provides a structured view of your revenue engine, strengthens your narrative and gives investors confidence that growth is grounded in a system rather than hope.

Can this replace a traditional GTM audit?

In most cases, yes. The combination of the GTM Score, the Intelligence Report and the modular deep-dives delivers the clarity leaders usually seek from an audit, but without meetings, interviews or consulting overhead.

Do you support implementation?

Yes. Advisory support helps you apply GRIP to decisions, prioritisation and day-to-day execution. It gives teams structure, removes uncertainty and creates a predictable cadence for improvement.

Do you offer fixed pricing or time-based pricing?

The GTM Score is free. The Report and Modules have fixed prices. Advisory can be fixed-scope or a simple monthly retainer. We believe in pricing outcomes, not hours.

How do payments work?

For fixed-scope work, payment is made at the start of the engagement. For advisory support, billing follows a straightforward monthly structure with no long-term lock-in unless agreed. Everything is designed to be clear, predictable and easy for your finance team.

Do we need a long-term contract?

No. You can start with a single diagnostic or module. Advisory support is offered month-to-month with clear deliverables and a structured cadence. You extend only if the work continues to deliver value.

Can we start small and expand later?

Yes. Most companies begin with the GTM Score and the Intelligence Report. Modules and advisory support are added only where GRIP shows the highest leverage, ensuring every investment creates meaningful movement.

Do you work with procurement and legal teams?

Yes. Caugia provides clear scopes, structured deliverables and simple agreements. This reduces review cycles and ensures compliance with procurement requirements.

Is there a refund or satisfaction guarantee?

Caugia provides fixed-scope work with clearly defined outputs. If something is not delivered as agreed or a deliverable is incomplete, it is corrected immediately at no additional cost. If a team disagrees with the insights or recommendations, that is not a basis for a refund, which is standard practice in consulting.
