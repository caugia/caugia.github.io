Data & Insights — FAQ

Every team has different pipeline numbers. Will this tell us why we cannot get a single source of truth?

Yes. The Data Strategy engine evaluates whether you have standardized KPI definitions across functions. The Pipeline Analytics engine checks if pipeline metrics are calculated consistently. The Data Culture engine measures whether teams trust and use shared data or maintain their own shadow spreadsheets. A measurement score below 50 means you are operating in a black box — decisions are based on opinions, not shared facts.

What benchmarks does this module compare us against?

Data infrastructure completeness, insight-to-action speed (how quickly data becomes a decision), KPI standardization across functions, and data culture maturity. A measurement score below 50 flags a black box operation. Above 70 indicates a strong data foundation. The diagnostic also measures customer intelligence depth, marketing analytics maturity, and predictive AI adoption readiness.

What specific insights will the diagnostic reveal?

Six engines cover the data-to-decision chain: Data Strategy measures whether you have a documented data plan with clear ownership. Customer Intelligence evaluates how well you use customer data for segmentation and personalization. Pipeline Analytics scores pipeline reporting accuracy and consistency. Marketing Analytics assesses attribution quality and campaign ROI measurement. Predictive AI evaluates readiness for predictive modeling and AI-driven insights. Data Culture measures whether teams actually use data in daily decisions or default to gut feel.

How is this different from a BI tool implementation or data audit?

A BI tool gives you dashboards. A data audit checks quality. This diagnostic measures whether your data infrastructure actually drives better decisions — whether insights reach decision-makers fast enough, whether KPIs are standardized across functions, and whether your organization has the data culture to act on what the numbers show. Tools and clean data are necessary but not sufficient; this measures the full insight-to-action chain.

Can 60 questions really assess our data and insights maturity?

The 6-engine architecture covers infrastructure through analytics through culture. Each engine has 10 questions scoring against maturity levels from basic (spreadsheet-driven, inconsistent definitions) to advanced (predictive, standardized, culturally embedded). The per-engine breakdown shows exactly where your data chain breaks — whether it is strategy, customer intelligence, pipeline analytics, marketing measurement, AI readiness, or cultural adoption.
